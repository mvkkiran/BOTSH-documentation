Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

Building Topology Ontology for Smart Homes (BOTSH) is an interoperable ontology developed based on BOT and customized specifically to the domain of smart homes. The current version v.1.0 contains 8 classes and 15 object properties. It is completely aligned with Smart Home and Building Ontology (BOSH) which is a domain ontology for smart homes that describes concepts like sensors, actuators, appliances, observations and measurements.

Building Storeyc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/botsh#BuildingStorey

A part of the physical world or a virtual world that is inherently both located in this world and having a 3D spatial extent. A botsh:BuildingStorey is contained in one or more buildings, and is intended to contain one or more spaces that are horizontally connected. Storeys of a building are connected by means of vertical connections such as elevators and stairs. A botsh:BuildingStorey encompasses both zones above and below ground, for example, a building with 21 floors above ground, one ground floor and 3 basements is equal to the sentence: A building has 25 instances of botsh:BuildingStorey.

Zonec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/botsh#Zone

A part of the physical world or a virtual world that is inherently botsh located in this world and has a 3D spatial extent; Sub-classes of botsh:Zone include botsh:Site, botsh:Building, botsh:BuildingStorey, or botsh:Space. An instance of botsh:Zone can contain other botsh:Zone instances, making it possible to group or subdivide zones. An instance of botsh:Zone can be adjacent to or intersecting other botsh:Zone instances. Finally, a botsh:Zone can instantiate three relations to botsh:BuildingElement, which are either contained in (botsh:containsElement), adjacent to it (botsh:adjacentElement) or intersecting (botsh:intersectingElement).

has elementop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/botsh#hasElement

Links a Zone to an Element that is either contained in, adjacent to or intersecting with the Zone. The intended use of this relationship is not to be stated explicitly, but to be inferred from its sub-properties. It will, for example, allow one to query for all the doors of a building given that they have an adjacency to spaces of the building.
